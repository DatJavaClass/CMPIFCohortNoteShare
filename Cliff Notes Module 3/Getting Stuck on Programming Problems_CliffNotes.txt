================================================================
THE PREMISE
================================================================
Getting stuck is INEVITABLE. The instructor describes spending
hours debugging, searching online, staying up late — and it being
maddening. The question this lecture answers: when stuck, what
should you actually do?

================================================================
WHY STRUGGLE ISN'T WASTED TIME
================================================================
Those hours stuck aren't (mostly) wasted, because you're LEARNING
how to accomplish something — so next time you're less lost.

Instructor's personal habit: when he works through a stuck point,
he writes quick notes with example code in a reference document
for later reuse. Offered as ONE method, explicitly not claimed as
the best or universal — find what works for you.

================================================================
THE KEY DISTINCTION — HOW YOU USE RESOURCES
================================================================
What to AVOID: jumping to resources in ways that just hand you the
output without making you think or learn.

Two copy-paste traps called out:
  - Generative AI — asking direct questions and pasting exact
    answers
  - Posted online solutions — common for intro-level material,
    often copyable nearly wholesale

Both mean you're not learning much. (The instructor promises a
later video on what counts as GOOD use of resources like
generative AI.)

================================================================
THE ARGUMENT FOR DOING IT THE HARD WAY
================================================================
  - You're here to LEARN, not just to get a grade. Someone (you
    or another investor) is investing in this degree — the point
    is to actually know something at the end.
  - Struggling more early on pays off later in what you actually
    know.
  - JOB-MARKET REALITY: data scientists and programmers are
    tested in interviews on code they can produce THEMSELVES —
    not what generative AI produces for them.
  - To get hired in data science you'll need to either come up
    with and code solutions yourself, OR at minimum understand
    and correct what generative AI produces.

================================================================
HIGH-VALUE TAKEAWAYS
================================================================
  - Being stuck is normal and is itself part of learning — don't
    treat it as failure.
  - Don't short-circuit learning by copy-pasting answers (from AI
    or posted solutions); that defeats the purpose of the course.
  - Keep your own reference notes of solutions you work out.
  - The long-term payoff (real skill, interview performance,
    employability) justifies the short-term struggle.
  - Minimum bar even when using AI: be able to understand and
    correct its output, not just paste it.
