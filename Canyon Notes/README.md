# Canyon Notes

## What these are

A Canyon Note is the deepest tier of study note in this repo. A Cliff Note is terse; a Cliff
Jumper Note weaves one module into a single lesson; a **Canyon Note weaves the entire half-semester
into one connected, illustrated lesson** - deeper and layered, enriched with worked examples,
callouts, sidebars, and real graphs, but never padded.

**`CMPIF2100_Part1_Canyon_Notes.pdf`** covers **Modules 1 through 8** (Foundations and the Pipeline,
Programming in Python, Quantifying Uncertainty, NumPy, pandas, and Visualization) in a single
35-page document. The six parts are tied together by twelve "through-line" threads that show how the
same handful of ideas (the data table, the list comprehension, the `1/sqrt(n)` law, "axis is the
direction you collapse," and more) reappear across every module.

## How it is checked: the Double Diamond

The Cliff Jumper notes used a single Diamond QC. The Canyon Note uses two diamonds in sequence:

1. **Diamond 1 (Truth).** An author drafts a locked list of facts; two independent checkers hunt for
   errors (one tracing every claim to the source material, the other running every line of code and
   every number); a meta-checker reviews them. The result is a verified, locked spine.
2. **Diamond 2 (Clarity).** The verified facts are enriched with examples, traces, analogies, and
   graphs - and every new example and figure is itself re-run and checked against the locked facts,
   so a clear explanation can never quietly install a wrong idea.
3. **Release gate.** Before publishing, the whole assembled document is reviewed again on truth,
   clarity, and presentation (including grayscale and color-blind legibility).

Every number and every figure in the PDF was executed on a real machine, not recalled.

## Confidence

All six parts are High to Very High. The executable material (Python, NumPy, pandas, statistics, and
all twelve graphs) was run on the standard Anaconda stack; the conceptual material was traced to
source and adversarially reviewed. Four "answer the course's way" notes flag places where the course
states something the loose way (the undefined-variable error class, the confidence-interval reading,
the text-column dtype, and an array-repr quirk).

## A note of caution

This is a study aid, not a textbook. Use it to understand and review, but check anything important
against the actual course material, especially before a quiz.
